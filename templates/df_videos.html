VIDEO DEEPFAKES

Video deepfakes are similar to image deepfakes, it is possible to:

  • swap

    face of person in a video

  • morph

    their face with another face

  • manipulate

    the video to add/remove/change features and accessories to a person

Creating a person for a video would be needlessly too difficult and we use

facial reenactment

instead

Facial reenactment

If face was a mask we can swap and adjust how we see fit, facial reenactment would the act of

wearing

this mask and

acting

with it

How does it work?

The AI gets

picture(s)

or

video(s)

of the person who will have their face reenacted

Then we have to provide either video or audio that we want reenacted

The product is a video, where the original person is either

  • moving like the person in video from step 2

  • or their lips are matching what is said in the audio from step 2

Why do this?

Matching lips of a speaker to voice is useful for in dubbing process in movies, commercials etc.

Using an avatar that moves as the original person can be useful to preserve anonymity online, e.g. during investigative interviewing

Making a synthesized virtual characters move can be used in the movie and streaming industry, or it could give graphical representation virtual assistants (Siri, Alexa etc.)

What to look out for with video deepfakes?

Spreading videos with altered or completely manipulated content is an easy way to spread disinformation

Stealing someone's identity can be used to exploit applications that use one's face to verify identity such as gambling portals or internet banking applications

How can it work in practice?

Scenario 1:

  1. A disgruntled employee seeks revenge on their boss, aiming to damage their professional reputation by releasing a fake video of their boss

  2. The employee downloads videos of the boss's public speeches and presentations available online and leverages deepfake facial reenactment technology to create the fake

  3. They superimpose the boss's face onto a manipulated video, making it appear as though they are endorsing unethical practices

  4. The employee strategically releases the manipulated video on social media, making it seem like a recent public statement made by the boss

  5. The video gains traction, causing public outrage and tarnishing the boss's reputation, leading to potential professional consequences for the targeted individual

Scenario 2:

  1. A dissatisfied individual, nursing grievances against their ex-partner who has recently found a new relationship, decides to retaliate

  2. The person collects photos and videos of their ex-partner from social media and personal archives

  3. Utilizing deepfake facial manipulation, they create misleading videos suggesting the ex-partner engaging in inappropriate behavior with someone else

  4. The individual sends the manipulated videos to the ex-partner's new partner and their mutual friends, creating strain on the recently formed relationship

  5. The dissatisfied individual watches as the manipulated content creates turmoil, achieving their goal of sabotaging the new relationship
