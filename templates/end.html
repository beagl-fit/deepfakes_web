FOOD FOR THOUGHT

As indicated in the previous sections, the individual techniques of deepfake creation can be used together to create even more potent deepfakes!

For example, an attacker can:

  • swap your face on an incriminating photo and manipulate your expression and facial features

  • take an image and voice of your boss and use it to impersonate them in a FaceTime call or during a Zoom conference

  • take a video of a politician and completely alter what they are speaking about

Though presenting deepfakes online as real information can spread misinformation, calling real information deepfakes can muddle the waters just as much!

Advices to defend yourself against deepfakes:

  1. Analyse the content you are viewing

    • Deepfakes created with image generators often produce “wonky” fingers, smudges, and other oddities not found in authentic photos.

    • It is often a good idea to look at the eyes. Deepfakes often have irregular or no blinking patterns and can lack light reflections in eyes.

    • Zoom in! Look for digital anomalies, odd skin tones, and smudges between face and background.

    • Assess movement quality. Deepfakes can have movement that looks robotic, lacks tongue movement, or has mismatched lips.

    • Verify that the video or voice clip comes from a known and trusted source.

  2. Don't automatically believe people are who they sound and/or look like during online or phone conversations

    • Especially during conversations concerning money or sensitive information using a secret passwords or a special questions is advised!
