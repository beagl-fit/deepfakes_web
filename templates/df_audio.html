AUDIO DEEPFAKES

AI can do many things with audio recordings, but deepfakes are concerned with

creating

a voice and

converting

one voice into another

How does it work?

Instead of pictures, the AI is given

audio recordings

of a specific person for training

Once the training is completed, the AI is able to produce speech that:

  • can be

    easily understood

  • sounds

    indistinguishable

    from the source

Text-to-speech synthesis

Text-to-speech or TTS is a way to generate speech from a written text

Speech-to-speech conversion

Speech-to-speech or STS is a way to make one person sound as another

Why use TTS?

TTS adds accessibility to any text for people with visual impairments or other disabilities.

Easy and fast way to create long recordings, such as audiobooks.

TTS is the way digital assistants such as Siri or Alexa communicate with you.

Convenient way for streamers, youtubers etc. to not have to read messages such as donation messages.

For fun - it is a way to "make others" say silly things.

Why use STS?

Dubbing of movies, adds etc. can be done anyone and not change how the actors and actresses sound.

STS can be found in online games and voice parodies.

Creation of "AI covers" uses STS to make songs sound like they were sung by other artists.

Convenient way for streamers, youtubers etc. to not have to read messages such as donation messages.

For fun - it is a way to "make others" say silly things.

The main advantage of STS over TTS is the possibility to produce deepfakes in real-time, this means that the voice is converted as the person is speaking.

What to look out for?

Since the deepfake may sound identical to a person you know it can be easy to exploit this:

Vishing

  • an attack where the voice is used to gain victims trust and the aim can be fraud or stealing sensitive information

  • Imagine getting a call from someone that sounds like your boss/subordinate asking you for a password your company uses or a door code

  • Or your family member in distress asking you for some money

Scam

  • an attack where the aim is to defraud you of your money

  • often seen in form of adds, but could be even phone calls, podcasts...

  • a well-known person (and someone you trust) tells you to buy or to invest in something

  • only for you to see neither the money nor product/service you paid for

Biometrics

  • an attacker committing identity theft and using your voice can lead to these systems being unable to distinguish between you and them

  • things like smart locks with voice activation or telephone transactions, where voice is used to confirm ones identity are at risk

Using voice deepfakes is also easy way to spread

disinformation

, because an attacker can make anyone say things they never said and would never say

  • an attacker committing identity theft and using your voice can lead to these systems being unable to distinguish between you and them

  • misleading information, propaganda or racist remarks can be used to hurt anyone in the eyes of others or to support ones own agenda

TTS Scenario 2:

  1. A notorious fake news outlet aims to spread disinformation about a specific politician.

  2. The outlet downloads a video of the politician's speech and replicates their voice using deepfake technology.

  3. Crafting a script portraying the politician in a manner contrary to their beliefs, the outlet employs text-to-speech deepfake technology for narration.

  4. The outlet replaces the original audio in the video with the deepfake voice.

  5. The fabricated news story is published, supported by the deepfake, and outraged readers share the misinformation on social media.

  6. Other fake news outlets amplify the narrative, further damaging the politician's reputation and enhancing the fake story's credibility.

STS Scenario 1:

  1. A hacker, aiming to access a company's system, opts for voice conversion over traditional hacking methods.

  2. After identifying an employee with sufficient clearance, the hacker gathers information from the corporate website and social networks, including the target's habits.

  3. Discovering the target frequents a café, the hacker records the employee's speech covertly during conversations.

  4. Using segments of the recorded audio, the hacker analyzes and replicates the target's speech through voice conversion.

  5. Posing as the employee, the hacker calls the company's IT department, leveraging the replicated voice to request a forgotten passport.

  6. The small size of the company allows the hacker's ruse to succeed, as the IT specialist immediately provides the supposed coworker with the forgotten passport, granting unauthorized access.

STS Scenario 2:

  1. A crafty thief, driven by a desire for financial gain, targets a wealthy family by exploiting their familial concern.

  2. The thief thoroughly researches the target's social media, discovering that the parents are currently on vacation through images and videos.

  3. Utilizing the mother's voice heard in videos, the thief creates a deepfake and employs voice conversion technology to mimic her voice convincingly.

  4. Seizing a strategic moment, the thief places a call to the target, impersonating the distressed mother in need during the vacation.

  5. Employing voice conversion technology to mimic the mother's voice, the thief fabricates an emergency, urgently requesting financial assistance.

  6. Capitalizing on the emotional distress and urgency, the thief successfully convinces the target to wire them money, completing the deceptive scheme.
